80% OF DOGS & CATS HAVE GUM DISEASE BY AGE 3.

Periodontal disease is the most common infectious disease is both dogs and cats that affects over 87% of dogs and 70% of cats over 3 years of age, so it’s important to stay ahead of the curve to prevent unwanted pain for our small companions!

How to check your Dog for Periodontal Disease
A healthy pet starts with a healthy mouth Dental disease in dogs and cats is very common, opens in a new tab, and very dangerous. Left untreated, it can lead to major problems like heart disease and kidney failure. Regular dental care is critical to your pet’s health, but they can’t do it alone. Ovvet is here to help you brush up on your pet’s dental needs.
1
Check your pet's breath. If they have no recently eaten, bad breath can be the first signs of Periodontal disease, gingivitis & tooth decay.
2
Carefully feel around your pet's mouth for signs of problems: lumps, masses or sore spots.
3
Check your pet's gums for discolouration, bleeding or swelling.
4
Examin your pet's teeth for signs of discolouration, plaque and tartar build-up or signs of decay.
